 Long before hypertext became an industry buzzword, Flambeaux was marketing
 DOS Help! with its unique system of embedded cross-references and had
 received awards for excellence from the Computer Press Association.

──────────────────────────────── Chronology ─────────────────────────────────
  1984 ■ Flambeaux Software is founded by Jack Crone and Dan Rollins
         The word "flambeaux" is French -- literally "torches".

  1985 ■ ◄DOS Help!► (for DOS 2.0) is introduced

  1986 ■ DOS Help! receives a Computer Press Association award for
         excellence as an on-line tutorial
       ■ ◄Tech Help!► is introduced

  1987 ■ DOS Help! receives a second Computer Press Association award

  1988 ■ DOS Help! is designated Editor's Choice by PC Magazine
       ■ Ziff-Davis Publishing commissions Flambeaux to develop "The Best
         of Tech Help!" as a subscription premium for PC Tech Journal

  1989 ■ Ziff-Davis Publishing commissions Flambeaux to develop "The Best
         of DOS Help!" as a subscription premium for PC Computing
       ■ Phoenix Technologies Ltd. is licensed to distribute DOS Help! to
         the OEM market

  1990 ■ ◄xText►, the world's first practical hypertext system, is
         introduced
       ■ Multilingual DOS Help! is introduced, as Flambeaux translates
         its popular database into French, German, and Spanish editions

  1992 ■ xText Version 2.0 is released: major changes to user interface.

  1993 ■ The xText viewer is licensed for use by Microsoft in DOS 6.0
         (as the help system for Microsoft Anti-Virus).

  1994 ■ Flambeaux releases TECH Help! version 6.0
       ■ Flambeaux products are in use by millions of people worldwide
